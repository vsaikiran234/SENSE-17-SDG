About the project

The "SENSE - School Engagement for Nurturing SDG Education" project is an initiative designed to educate and engage school students in Vijayawada and Guntur about the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs). The project aims to assess the current level of SDG awareness among students through an initial interactive session and a pre-exposure exam. Students will then be introduced to an innovative learning tool developed by the Centre for Geospatial Technology, which offers a comprehensive overview of all 17 SDGs in an engaging and accessible format. After this exposure, a post-exposure exam will be conducted to measure the effectiveness of the tool in enhancing students' understanding of the SDGs. The initiative is significant in fostering awareness and encouraging students to take meaningful steps toward building a sustainable future, thus contributing to the global sustainability movement from a young age.

About Centre for Geospatial Technology

The Centre for Geospatial Technology (CGT) at SRM University-AP, Andhra Pradesh is an interdisciplinary research centre that aims to harness the power of geospatial science and provide solutions to industry and socially relevant problems while enabling students to learn about Geographical Information Science (GIS) through experiential learning. The mission of CGT is to spearhead the advancement of GIS technology through active collaborations with academic institutions and industries. The team of CGT, with vast experience in geospatial technology, offers services to various government and non-government agencies and also industries. Services of CGT range from education, training, and implementation of customised GIS solutions.
